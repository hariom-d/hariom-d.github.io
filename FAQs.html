Frequently Asked Questions

Q. Why learn to speak from Nalanda?
Nalanda: Our focus is mainly on spoken English and use unique techniques that will save massive time and effort of the learners.

Q. What is an "expression"?
Nalanda: We define "expression" as a reusable pattern in a communication. Speakers make a sentence NOT by a sequence of individual words. Instead, a sentence is made by a sequence of expressions(reusable patterns) and words.
Human brain tries to remember/use these reusable patterns as one single unit. This way the brain does not have to think/process hard to make a sentence quickly. Even the quality of the communication can be significantly improved by using the frequently used elegant expressions.

Q. Are Idioms/phrasal verbs same as expressions?
Nalanda: No. Idioms/Phrasal verbs are only a small subset of the expressions. Only popular expressions will be our focus to give the best value out of the time and effort of learners.

Q. How do you asses the popularity rating of the expressions?
Nalanda: We have placed trust in the native speakers and do not claim that the popularity rating is precise. There is no need to be very picky about popularity rating of some expressions.
We allow users to provide the comments on expressions and videos and would be happy to update the content based on the feedback.

Q. How many expressions do you have in your database and how can I learn so many expressions?
Nalanda: The total number of expressions will be in thousands. But we have divided the content based on user types(Student/Professional), their desired level(Basic/Advanced) and the popularity(High/Medium). We will NOT keep low popularity expressions as we believe that users should focus more on immidiately useful content to get better value out of their time and effort.

Q. Why do you recommend using expressions as prime technique to SPEAK a language?
Nalanda: Some learners make a mistake of spending a lot of time in learning difficult or not so commonly used words and focus less on simple yet powerful expressions. In conversation English, simplicity is the key and gives tremendous confidence to the learner.

Q. I can understand most of what native speakers speak in daily life/movies/soaps. Still I find it difficult to speak comfortably. Why?
Nalanda: Based on our understanding and the research done on internet, people tend to use words/expressions that they have used before and are comfortable about. The words they have never spoken before may cause confusion in the mind and the person starts stuttering and may become demotivated.
We advise learners to speak using simple words/expressions and avoid difficult and less popular words. Focus on a few commonly used contexts rather than trying to learn every context of the expression. Be selective and prudent in what you learn.
Keeping this in mind we have included commonly used contexts with the expressions.

Q. How did you prepare your database of expressions?
Nalanda: The expressions in our database are prepared by native English speakers. They provided common use cases of expressions and their popularity in spoken English. This will directly help learners in staying focussed and not wasting any time on stuff that is not so useful for their current needs.

Q. Why did you prepare videos when expressions provided good examples with context?
Nalanda: We understand that reading content from web pages can be boring at times and the content also may be open to misinterpretation. In order to provide a unique experience to our learners we have prepared highly engaging videos that will use some real-life themes to explain a number of expressions from our database.

Q. I have been advised by my teachers and friends that I should read newspapers/magazine/novels and watch English movies/soaps. Are they not effective?
Nalanda: All these resources are a great way to learn English. You need to decide your objective first and the amount of time you are willing to devote. If your aim is to speak English then how would you easily know whether a particular word/expression is more suitable for writing or used in very formal speeches? Some expressions in movies and soaps are slangs and using them without proper care can be a bit off-putting to your listeners.
In addition to movies/soaps, we recommend learning with Nalanda. Our content is developed within the boundaries of decency to avoid any embarrasement for you. Our popularity rating will further help you to choose the right content and be more productive in your learning journey.

Q. I want to learn industry specific terminology. Does your content for professionals cover that?
Nalanda: No. All our content is generic and is suitable for good soft skills needed in any industry.
